## Context

Protein synthesis simulations generate graphs with millions of nodes whose evaluation with classical methods takes hours. This project replaces traditional physicochemical scoring with a GNN trained to predict the viability of synthesis routes in seconds.

## Architecture

The model is a message-passing neural network (MPNN) with edge attention, trained with PyTorch Geometric. Neighborhood sampling runs on custom CUDA kernels to keep the full graph on GPU and avoid the transfer bottleneck.

## Results

- Spearman correlation of **0.91** against the reference physicochemical scoring.
- Evaluation time per route: from 40 minutes down to **11 seconds**.
- In production as a pre-filter for the screening pipeline, discarding 87% of unviable routes before the expensive simulation.
