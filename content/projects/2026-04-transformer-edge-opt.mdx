## Context

Bringing a transformer to a device with 512 MB of RAM and no dedicated accelerator requires rethinking the whole model, not just quantizing it. This project explores the practical limit of compression: how much can a model be reduced before the degradation becomes noticeable in intent-classification tasks?

## Approach

Three techniques applied in cascade, measured in isolation and combined:

1. **Structured distillation** into a student architecture with half the layers and grouped attention.
2. **Post-training int8 quantization** with percentile calibration over a representative corpus.
3. **Attention-head pruning** guided by activation variance on validation.

Inference runs on TensorFlow Lite with critical kernels rewritten in C++ for the embedding path.

## Current status

The project is in an **experimental** phase: the int8 variant reaches 94% of the teacher model's F1 with 9× less memory, but cold-start latency still exceeds the budget on lower-end devices. The next iteration explores selective per-layer fp16 quantization.
